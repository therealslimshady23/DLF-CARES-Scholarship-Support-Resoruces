Why School-Level Scholarships Matter

📊

High Dropout Rates

India's secondary school dropout rate averages 14–39% in high-risk states like Bihar, MP, Jharkhand, and Chhattisgarh.

👶

Early Intervention

DLF CARES' Class 1 entry point is the earliest among peers—intervening before dropout risk even forms.

💰

Financial Barriers

Financial constraints at Class 1–10 level are the primary structural driver of dropout across underserved communities.

🎓

Formative Years

School-level intervention—especially before Class 5—has the highest long-term impact on educational and career outcomes.

🌍

Inclusive Approach

DLF CARES is one of very few programmes starting at Class 1, open to all genders, with no caste, plant, or community restriction.

What DLF CARES Can Adopt

High-priority recommendations to strengthen impact and deliver greater value to communities we serve

💵

Direct Family Stipend

Inspired by: Coal India Scholarship

Monthly household stipend of ₹300–500 for active scholars' families to cover non-fee expenses like meals, stationery, and transport.

Priority: HIGH
👥

Dedicated Caregiver per Cohort

Inspired by: Mphasis VIDYA

Assign a trained mentor-guardian to each cohort of 20–25 scholars for continuity of pastoral care and early identification of at-risk students.

Priority: HIGH
🛠️

After-School Vocational Learning

Inspired by: JSW Umeed

Introduce skill-based learning modules from Class 6 in partnership with platforms like iDream or NSDC covering digital literacy, financial literacy, and communication.

Priority: HIGH
🌐

Open Online Application Portal

Inspired by: HDFC Parivartan

Launch a public-facing digital application portal open to all schools, not just partner schools. This widens the eligible student pipeline significantly.

Priority: MEDIUM
🔄

Multiple Application Cycles

Inspired by: HDFC Parivartan & Naandi Prerana

Introduce a mid-year application window to capture students who miss the primary cycle, ensuring no deserving student is left behind.

Priority: MEDIUM
🧒

Early Childhood Integration

Inspired by: UltraTech Cement CSR

Partner with anganwadis near partner schools to introduce a pre-Class 1 readiness programme, addressing school-readiness before dropout risk forms.

Priority: MEDIUM
🚌

Transport Support

Inspired by: Vedanta DAV Scholarship

Introduce a transport allowance or school bus tie-up for scholars commuting over 3km. This reduces absenteeism caused by travel barriers.

Priority: MEDIUM
👨‍👩‍👧

Parent Engagement Workshops

Research-backed Initiative

Conduct quarterly parent workshops at partner schools. Parental involvement directly correlates with reduced dropout rates and improved home learning support.

Priority: MEDIUM
👩‍🏫

Community Tutoring Model

Inspired by: Nanhi Kali

Deploy trained local women from scholar neighbourhoods as community tutors to provide after-school academic support within walking distance.

Priority: MEDIUM

Key Differentiators of DLF CARES

✓ Entry Point

Only program beginning at Class 1, intervening before dropout risk sets in. Nanhi Kali starts at Grade 6; Sitaram Jindal at Class 11.

✓ Support Depth

Beyond fee coverage, provides remedial classes, mental health counselling, ACER-based assessments, and career guidance. No peer program offers this full stack at school level.

✓ Inclusivity

Supports all genders from the outset. Nanhi Kali is exclusively girls-focused. Sitaram Jindal is open to all but begins too late.

✓ Continuity

Higher Education Initiative now follows scholars from Class 1 through undergraduate study—creating a rare end-to-end pipeline neither peer replicates.

✓ Geographic Reach

Presence in 21 states with strong focus on highest dropout-risk regions. Most peer programs are concentrated in 3–5 states.

✓ Scale Opportunity

With 4,247 active scholars across 21 states, DLF CARES has strong geographic presence with clear pathways to expand reach and increase cohort size.

Moving Forward

DLF CARES has built one of the most comprehensive school scholarship models in India. These recommendations are not about fixing what is broken—they are about deepening what already works and extending its reach to students who currently fall outside the programme's net.

By implementing these evidence-backed, school-level-specific improvements, DLF CARES can position itself as the definitive model for inclusive, holistic school-level CSR scholarships across India.
